  • (b) Explain the five range safety rules.
  • Always walk on a range. - Never run on a range because you can quicky run into the shooting field and could get shoot
  • Keep your arrows in your quiver until told to shoot. - Never take out your arrows because they are sharp and could hurt others.
  • Only release the bow string when an arrow is nocked and safely pointed toward the target. - When there is an arrow the bow will release its energy into it but when there is no arrow the bow releases the energy back into you.
  • Leave dropped arrows on the ground until instructed to retrieve them. - Even walking a small bit into the firing ground can be dangerous so you should wait until everyone is done shooting and you are allowed to retrieve the arrow.
  • If there is an emergency on the range immediately alert the Instructor. - If for example someone fell over onto the firing ground they could get shot quickly so always tell a range instructor immediately.
  • (c) Explain the four whistle commands used on the range.
  • Two blasts - Walk to the shooting line and prepare to start shooting but you may NOT pick up an arrow.
  • One blast - Start shooting once finished, put the bow back and go to the waiting line.
  • Three blasts - Go downrange and retrieve the arrows.
  • Four or more blasts - STOP firing immediately put the arrow back into the quiver and step back to the waiting line.
  • (d) Explain how to safely remove arrows from the target and return them to your quiver
  • Always pull from the side of the target so the arrow doesn't stab at you then grab them like scissors point down and just below your hand.

  • Target - Flat, open fields or inside ranges with circular bullseye targets
  • Field target - Natural terrain (wood, hils, trails) with paper targets
  • 3-D archery - Outdoor usually in forests with life size animal targets

  • (d) How the National Field Archery Association (NFAA) black-and-white field targets and blue indoor targets are scored.
  • Black-and-white targets - 5 scoring rings, X center (extra for tie braking), 5 points white inner ring, 4 points firest black ring, 3 points outer black ring
  • blue indoor targets - 5 scoring rings, X same as black-and-white, 5 points white inner ring, 4 points next ring (light blue), 3 points outer ring (darker blue)
